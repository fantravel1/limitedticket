Frequently Asked

Ticket Intelligence FAQ

Direct answers to the most common questions about getting impossible tickets. Sourced from real fan experiences and industry knowledge.

How do I get tickets for an event when it sells out instantly?

Start by registering for every official presale available — Verified Fan, artist presales, venue presales, and credit card presales. Set up your ticketing accounts and payment methods days before the drop. Use the LimitedTicket Readiness Checklist for your specific event to ensure you're fully prepared.

On sale day, log in early, have multiple devices ready (phone app + desktop browser), and don't refresh once you're in a queue. Many events also have official waitlists and resale through the original platform — these are your safest second chances.

When do event tickets go on sale (and what time zone)?

On-sale times vary by event and region. LimitedTicket's Drop Calendar tracks every major on-sale date globally with automatic time zone conversion. Most US concerts drop at 10 AM local venue time, UK events at 9-10 AM GMT, and international sports events follow their federation's schedule.

Always confirm the exact time on the official ticketing platform and set alerts through LimitedTicket's calendar sync feature.

What is the best legitimate way to get impossible tickets?

The best legitimate strategy combines preparation and timing: (1) Register for all available presales including Verified Fan, artist fan clubs, and credit card presales. (2) Complete your Readiness Checklist — accounts created, payment verified, ID ready. (3) Use our Drop Calendar to know the exact second tickets become available. (4) Have a backup plan: official resale platforms, waitlists, and less popular show dates or cities.

LimitedTicket's Playbook provides detailed, ethical strategies for every major event type.

What is Verified Fan and how do I use it correctly?

Verified Fan is Ticketmaster's program that filters real fans from bots and scalpers. Register during the open window (usually 1-2 weeks before the sale), verify your identity, and wait for a confirmation code. If selected, you'll receive a unique access code and a designated purchase window.

Tips: register early, use your real information, ensure your Ticketmaster account is in good standing, and have your payment method saved. Being waitlisted doesn't mean you're out — codes are often released in waves.

How do presales work and which ones actually matter?

Presales are early purchase windows before the general public on-sale. The most valuable are: (1) Verified Fan — often has the best inventory, (2) Artist/Fan Club presales — reward loyal fans, (3) Venue presales — sometimes overlooked and less competitive, (4) Credit card presales (Amex, Chase, Citi) — dedicated allocations.

Register for ALL of them to maximize your chances. Each presale has its own ticket allocation, so more presales = more opportunities.

How do I avoid ticket scams for high-demand concerts?

Follow these rules: (1) Only buy from official platforms or their authorized resale marketplaces. (2) Never buy through social media DMs, Craigslist, or random websites. (3) If the price seems too good to be true, it is. (4) Use payment methods with buyer protection. (5) Verify the ticket's barcode and transfer method before paying.

Red flags include sellers who want wire transfers, won't show proof of tickets, or pressure you to act immediately. Check our Scam Shield for event-specific warnings.

Is StubHub, Viagogo, or SeatGeek safe for event tickets?

StubHub and SeatGeek are generally considered safer resale platforms with buyer guarantees. Viagogo has faced regulatory action in multiple countries — check their guarantee terms carefully.

Even on reputable platforms, verify: Does the venue allow transferred/resale tickets? Are there ID requirements matching the original buyer? What's the platform's guarantee if entry is refused? Our Safe Resale Comparison tool rates every major platform by event type and region.

Why are event tickets so hard to get?

Several factors create extreme scarcity: (1) Demand vastly exceeds supply — a stadium holds 60,000 but millions want to attend. (2) Bots and scalpers capture significant inventory despite defenses. (3) Limited tour dates concentrate demand. (4) Presale allocations reduce general sale inventory. (5) Dynamic pricing raises costs as demand spikes.

LimitedTicket's LimitedScore rates each event from 1 to 100 so you know exactly what you're facing before you try.

What is the best device setup for ticket drops (without cheating)?

Maximize your chances legally: Use 2-3 devices — your phone with the official app plus a desktop browser. Clear cookies and cache beforehand. Use a fast, stable internet connection (avoid public Wi-Fi). Log into your account on all devices before the drop. Have payment info saved. Don't refresh once you're in a queue.

Having a family member or friend try simultaneously with their own account is perfectly legitimate and dramatically improves your odds.

What should I do if I missed the ticket drop?

Don't panic — you still have options: (1) Join the official waitlist immediately. (2) Check the official resale/exchange platform — tickets get listed regularly as plans change. (3) Monitor for additional show dates. (4) Check verified resale platforms closer to the event when prices often drop. (5) Consider less popular cities or dates on the same tour.

Follow our Last-Minute Strategy guide for proven approaches to finding tickets after sellout.
